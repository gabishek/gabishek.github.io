My Design Process

Define the Requirements

I kick things off by pinpointing the project's core business needs, nailing down requirements, and getting a grasp of the desired outcomes, timelines, and team expectations. Keeping the lines of communication open with the team is paramount. At this stage, I also begin sketching out initial thoughts on the puzzle we're about to solve.

Discover the Issues

This is where I dive deep, collecting intel, engaging in discussions, and delving into the intricacies of the problem at hand. I might sift through user feedback or investigate why the current design is the way it is. If we're short on budget or time for a full-blown user research expedition, I'll go for informal interviews to avoid shooting in the dark.

Interpret the Results

The decoding phase is when I define who our users are, what makes them tick, and where it hurts. I craft personas, chart journey maps, sketch out storyboards, and whip up problem statements or refine existing ones. This is also the stage where we set our project goals and the metrics we'll use to measure success. It's often an eye-opening journey.

Ideate over Solutions

The ideation phase – my personal playground. I get everyone in on the brainstorming action: developers, fellow designers, stakeholders, even the friendly neighborhood customer support team. We let the ideas flow freely, aiming for quantity over quality at this stage

Prototype

Now, it's time to give shape to those brilliant ideas. I create an interactive prototype, whether it's a snazzy high-fidelity Figma masterpiece or a functional website, depending on the project's rhythm and testing needs.

Test, Iterate and Test Again

In the final stretch, I put our prototype to the test with real users. Sometimes it's a swift, informal check, while other times it's a full-blown usability testing extravaganza. The results serve as our guiding light, showing us what needs fine-tuning and enhancing. It's all about iterating to perfection..

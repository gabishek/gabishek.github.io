Find Jobs - We are Here for You

Description

Find Jobs - We are Here for You is a mobile-based application targeting formerly incarcerated individuals to find jobs, chat with employers, and engage with the community.

Role: Product Designer / No-Code

Team Size: 1

Platform: Mobile Application

Tool Stack: Figma / Adobe XD / Microsoft PowerPoint / Adobe Illustration

Duration: August 2022 - February 2023

Link: Click Here

Goal

The goal of the app is to target only formerly incarcerated individuals and ease the process of finding and applying for jobs.

Discover

Initial Problem Discovery

Why is there such a large gap between job opportunities and incarcerated individuals?

×

Before initiating the design process and making any modifications, I sought to validate the data and conduct further research. I was surprised by the findings!

Competitve Analysis

Since I had a lot of data and my goal was saturated again, I wanted to explore what was currently successful and what could be improved.

I examined both direct and indirect competitors of the platform, aiming to facilitate and streamline the job search and application process. After evaluating numerous platforms, I finalized the top five, three of which are direct competitors and two are indirect competitors. Honest Jobs stands out as the leading platform, offering nearly all the features required. However, it lacks a mobile application and community chat functionality.

User Interview / Quantative Research

It was a huge challenge to identify formerly incarcerated individual, but I successfully identified 20 ex-inmates employed across various industries. I conducted in-person interviews with them, consisting of 30 questions (Open Ended, Close Ended and MCQs). While I desired more participants, these interviews provided valuable insights into the job search platform and process.

The picture and the result which are presented below are just few questions, after I was almost sure what was the major problem and how can the solutions be formulated.

Here are the summarized user preferences based on the survey:

  1. 75% of ex-inmates prefer a dedicated job search portal focusing solely on incarcerated individuals.
  2. 60% of ex-inmates seek to connect with former inmates to gain insights on discussing past convictions appropriately within the discussion community.
  3. 55% of ex-inmates prefer an intuitive and minimal design and require it to be a mobile application.
  4. 35% of ex-inmates seek a seperate section to provide detailed explanations of conviction history.
  5. 30% of ex-inmates prioritize simple and easy onboarding with convenient search and filtering options.
  6. 25% of ex-inmates wish to directly chat with employers/hiring managers before applying for jobs due to privacy concerns.

Analyzing the data

Now it was time to process and analyze the data to find one main theme with more than 100 data points from 20 formerly incarcerated individuals with 6 different insights.

The conclusion was to build a mobile-based application targeting solely incarcerated individuals to find jobs, chat with employers, and get connected with former inmates to gain insights.

Define

In this step, I will be creating a persona, an empathy map, a customer journey map, and finally coming up with a problem statement.

Persona and User Story

Now I moved forward to create a persona, as persona is a way to imagine who I would be designing for. I outlined their goals, needs, pain points, tech-savviness, and motivation. This was followed by a brief introduction and comprehensive details about the persona.

Meet Jamal. He lives in St Louis and wants to restart again and break the cycle of incarceration.

Empathy Map

What does a typical day look like for Jamal? What does he value and think about? Whether his actions align with his thoughts?

Jamal aims to find steady work and break free from past struggles. However, he feels anxious and unsure about how to do it, especially with jobs that ask for a clean record, and is wondering about an application dedicated only to individuals like Jamal.

Problem Statement

After completing all the research, I can deduce that ex-inmates need a mobile-based application targeting solely incarcerated individuals to find jobs, chat with employers, and get connected with former inmates to gain insights.

  1. How might we boost the confidence and trust of incarcerated individuals in the mobile application?
  2. How might we design the platform to be simple and easy to use for non-tech-savvy ex-inmates?
  3. How might we design the platform to protect user privacy and avoid stigma for candidates?
  4. How might we incorporate community and chat features that would make ex-inmates more likely to engage with and use the app regularly?

Ideate / Develop

This is the most creative section of the design process; in this section, I came up with solutions and created an information architecture, followed by sketches, mid-fidelity wireframes, usability testing, and a UI kit.

Information Architecture

There will be 3 journeys in information architecture that will be analyzed during usability testing.

  1. Onboarding Experience: This flow emphasizes the user’s seamless onboarding experience on the app, followed by job suggestions tailored to their profile.
  2. Applying for Jobs: This flow highlights the user’s job application process, emphasizing its intuitive and minimalist design.
  3. Search and Chat: This flow enables ex-inmates to participate in discussions with community members and facilitates chatting with employers to enhance their confidence before applying for jobs.

Sketches

Following the information architecture, I drafted my ideas with pen and pencil while considering the user's challenges and motivations.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

From initial sketches to mid-fidelity screens, I began by mapping out all the necessary pages for the three user journeys. While additional secondary pages emerged, I prioritized key pages from the information architecture. I allocated sections for each element and refined the details accordingly.

Usability Testing

I revisited all the participants, conducting in-person usability testing with 12 individuals, each session lasting approximately 45 minutes. The tests were moderated with the major focus on three journeys, with users interacting with the app in real-time via the Figma mobile app, mirroring it from the desktop.

The objective of the test:

  1. Observe if the participants can accomplish the specified task. If they make a mistake, can they recover?
  2. Observe the reactions of the participants when they navigate the app.
  3. Identify areas of the app that require a change based on the follow-up Q&A and reactions to enhance user performance and satisfaction.

Based on the feedback received, I revisited the design file to bridge the gap between my understanding of the users and their actual experiences.

UI KIT / STYLE GUIDES

What kind of feeling do I want ex-inmates to experience while using Find Jobs? I aim for ex-inmates to feel inspired and stable, symbolized by the color blue. The combination of blue and white also represents purity. Blue is the primary color in the application, supported by a few other shades. I used sans font for better legibility and to create a friendly and inviting environment.

Deliver / Reflection

Edge Cases?

These are two major edge cases that I have to figure out and solve in the coming days:

  • I have to conduct further research from a business perspective to ensure a balanced approach.
  • I have to investigate government policy that affects both businesses and users and make the app more refined to adhere to both policies.

What Next?

I have a huge list of features to add to the application and I want to work on that!

  • Incorporate an application status feature for ex-inmates, allowing them to monitor the progress of their application.
  • Integrate a feature for scheduling virtual interviews within the app for ex-inmates.
  • Implement a skill test to identify the specific skills of ex-inmates and suggest jobs accordingly.
  • Create a dashboard for employers to view the number of applications received via the application.
  • Include a blog section where successful ex-inmates can share their journey through inspiring articles.
  • Better adhere to WACG standards.

Major Learning

Through regular usability testing, I gained crucial insight into how to translate user data and feedback into tangible designs that align with UX Design principles. By designing and building what the community needs, I gained a deeper understanding of designing within the constraints of feasibility and effort.

There is a huge list of learning, I subconsciously thought of what the final design would be even before I started, however, after the research and defining phase, that completely changed. Saying so I would like to add the top 4 learnings:

  • Heuristic Evaluation and moderated usability testing helped me to understand what end users will feel when they use the app.
  • I learned how to think intentionally about every element of the project and how it can contribute to the result.
  • I gained a deeper understanding of designing within the constraints of feasibility and effort.
  • Problem that is defined correctly, almost contains the final solution.
